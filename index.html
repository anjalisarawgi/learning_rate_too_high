I'm a Master's of Statistics and Data Science student at LMU, Munich. I really enjoy working with domain experts to create machine learning based solutions to their problems. I like exploring different fields because I think each one comes with some challenges, and I learn the most when I am faced with these challenges.

I also believe that AI/ML models are powerful tools, and helping people from different domains use these technologies is fulfilling for me. So, I make conscious efforts to find opportunities where I get to push myself to learn, apply what I know, and contribute, with an attempt to always keep my learning curve in an increasing trend.

Some things I have worked/am working on are:

  • Graph Neural Networks -> I am using GNNs to detect functional subcircuits (e.g., Sbox, key expansion) in digital circuit netlists. This is a part of my master's thesis.
  • Low-Resource ML -> I collaborated with a historian to build a handwritten text model for 18th and 19th century manuscripts to enable automatic transcriptions (repo to be released soon!) [cannot say alot about this since we have a anonymous conference submission]
  • Psycological Research -> At LMU, Munich, I worked for the Phone study project (https://phonestudy.org/en/), where they capture and study mobile sensing data for finding methods to understand the users mental health. Here, very interestingly I worked on the keyboard data, where I prepared the data, and created features to see if users typing behaviour can indicate depression.
  • Cultural bias -> Very recently, I have been very intrigued with the cultural stereotypes most large language models capture, and I have been trying to infer these behaviours to see for another possible research direction i could work on this winter of 2025. Particularly, I am curious to evaluate how these models handle smaller or less represented regions, for example Nepal. I think it would be very interesting to see if we can identify and solve for the biases in the dataset it was trained on itself.
